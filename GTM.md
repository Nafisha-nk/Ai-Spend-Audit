# GTM

The exact first target user is a seed-to-Series A technical founder or head of engineering at a 5-50 person software startup that already pays for multiple AI tools across engineering, product, and go-to-market. They are not looking for a generic “AI cost management platform.” They are looking at a credit card bill, a Linear thread about too many AI subscriptions, or a Slack message from finance asking why AI spend doubled.

Right before they want this tool, they Google or scroll through phrases like “Cursor vs Copilot cost,” “ChatGPT Team worth it,” “Claude Max team pricing,” “OpenAI API bill too high,” or “how much should startups spend on AI tools.” They also see founder posts about AI bills quietly becoming a new SaaS line item.

The strongest zero-budget channels are specific founder and engineering communities: Hacker News launch posts, r/SaaS, r/startups, r/ExperiencedDevs, Indie Hackers, the Vercel/Next.js and Supabase Discords, founder Slack groups, and X replies under posts about Cursor, Claude Code, Copilot, and OpenAI API cost spikes. I would not post “we built a tool.” I would post one concrete teardown: “I audited a 12-person startup paying for Cursor Business + Copilot Business + ChatGPT Team. They could save $X/mo without losing capability.”

First 100 users in 30 days: interview 10 founders, convert those into 3 anonymized spend teardowns, post one teardown every 3 days, DM 50 founders who recently posted about AI tooling, and reply to 30 relevant X/HN/Reddit threads with a calculator link and the exact insight. I would also ask Credex to share anonymized credit-market pricing ranges that normal founders cannot easily access. That is the unfair channel: Credex sees real discount opportunities from overforecasted credits, so the app can tell users not just “switch plan,” but “this spend is big enough to negotiate credits.”

Week-1 traction that suggests it works: 75 completed audits, 20 captured emails, 5 high-savings consultation clicks, 3 replies asking for manual review, and at least one user sharing their public audit URL without being asked.
